List of recommendations

The 9 recommendations have been sorted into three categories according to their purpose. However, you can feel free to explore them in any way you prefer.

Introducing the use of simulations

1

Use color blindness simulations to introduce the accessibility discourse in your team's day-to-day
2

Training is necessary, but don't wait to be an expert to get started

Using simulations for interface and gameplay inspection

3

Use color blindness simulations in combination with game accessibility guidelines
4

Choose the simulation tool that makes your work process less difficult
5

Compare the simulated versions with the original version of your game
6

Keep an eye out for visual feedback and other elements that aren't always visible on the game screen
7

Fix identified problems, even cosmetic ones, before moving forward

Using simulations for tests with players with colorblindness

8

Use the simulations to strategically plan the focus of research with players with color blindness

8.1. Let the participant explore freely at first

8.2. Look for issues that may be invisible to players with color blindness

8.3. Use pain points identified with simulations as talking points
9

Explore with players with color blindness the meanings of the colors chosen for the game

These recommendations were the main outcome of an extensive research on the tools used to create chromatically accessible games, conducted between 2020 and 2023 by:
