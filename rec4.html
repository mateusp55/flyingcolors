4. Choose the simulation tool that makes your work process less difficult

It is important that the use of simulations is comfortable so that they are integrated into the desi process. To do this, you need to choose carefully between the various options available.

There are three most common types of color blindness simulations: on a desktop screen, on a smartphone screen or in augmented reality. Studies indicate that each of these types is best suited to different activities in the game creation process.

PROBLEM SCENARIO

You have used color blindness simulations before but find it difficult to integrate them into your daily work. You end up not using simulations often because they seem to disrupt your workflow, requiring the use of different programs and devices.

Simulations on a desktop screen are more suitable for activities such as interface prototyping and asset creation (Pre-production and Production stages), as the professional can already apply the simulation on the same device theyâ€™re working with.

The other two types are better suited to more advanced stages (production, testing and beta stages) where there are already playable prototypes that can be tested on the target device. See the table below.

USE SCENARIO

You start exploring different options of color blindness simulations on the desktop platform to find one that allows you to simulate various types of color blindness and compare them tothe original version and that doesn't require you to leave Figma. You also begin to explore the possibility of using simulations on other platforms. Testing the gameplay of your games with Android's native color blindness simulation has become a common practice for the team. Thus, you started using simulations from creating assets for quick queries to applying more complex interface inspections.

TIPS

  • Explore different tools until you find the one that best suits your needs. You can start your search from this list of color blindness simulations.
  • Consider carefully: tools integrated to the programs you already use are ideal because they disturb your workflow less, however, they do not always give you the best possibilities (e.g.: Photoshop only offers simulations of Protanopia and Deuteranopia).
  • Try using different simulation modalities at different stages of the process. This may allow you to identify new areas in the game that need attention.
